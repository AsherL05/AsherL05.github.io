Fishing Technology and its Future

Technological advances have been influencing almost every major economic field in recent years, so how has fishing been affected?

The seafood industry makes up ~10% of the world's jobs, and fishing hasn't made many major advances in recent history, so imcorporating new fishing technology could be detrimental to the world's economy, along with exponentially decreasing the world's sea population. Even with these limitations and expenses there have been a few improvements in recent years. While the future of technology is incredibly hard to predict, I believe that fishing technology will have a major breakthrough soon, once the drive for technological advancements in other fields decreases.

The Smart Boat

Smart Boat to Catch some Dumb Fish

The Smart Boat, similar to your smart phone, is equipped with a couple technological advantages. For example, this boat is equipped with multiple cameras made to take high definition photos of below the water. This can help to quickly find better fishing spots, or just to take some pretty pictures of some funny fish. Next, the boat is equipped with hydraulic and rotation sensors. These sensors can detect and record any movement close by, and can even be used to record an epic catch. Lastly, the boat is equipped with a electronic monitoring computer, which records the vessel's telemetry and video data. If you didn't know, telemetry data calculates the distance the vessel is from the surface.
